Greg Goelzhauser

I'm an associate professor in the Department of Political Science at Utah State University. I study law and courts with an emphasis on institutional design, marginalized group representation, and judicial decision making. Current projects address the constitutionality of police shootings, qualified immunity from civil rights lawsuits, the Supreme Court's shadow docket, and state court diversification.

Intersectional Representation

For new state supreme court seatings, appointments are more likely to yield women and men of color, elections are more likely to yield white men, and white women are seated similarly across selection institutions.

  • Read More

Diversifying State Supreme Courts

Many states still haven't seated their first Black justice; those with partisan elections are slower to do so and those with nominating commissions are faster. Selection systems are generally not associated with gender diversification.

  • Read More

Merit Selection Outcomes

Judicial nominating commissions are less likely to nominate women and more likely to nominate gubernatorial copartisans and people with judicial experience. Governors are more likey to appoint copartisans and graduates from the state's flagship, or a higher ranked, law school.

  • Read More

Judicial Ambition

Under merit selection, judges with better bar evaluations are more likely to apply for promotion; attorneys who are gubernatorial copartisans are more likely to apply for a first judgeship.

Opinion Clarity

State supreme courts produce similarly readable opinions across retention institutions, even in salient cases.

Case Disposition Speed

Trial court judges facing partisan elections dispose of certain criminal and civil cases faster than colleagues in the same state who face retention elections.

Classifying Selection Institutions

Inferences about selection system performance are impacted by differences in defining merit selection, ambiguous theoretical arguments, and failure to treat selection as a judge- rather than state-level phenomenon where applicable.
