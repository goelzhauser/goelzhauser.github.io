Greg Goelzhauser

I'm an associate professor in the Department of Political Science at Utah State University. I'm also co-founder and co-director of the Data and Politics Lab, which is a research incubator for an interdisciplinary group of faculty and students at Utah State University using social data analytics to study important political questions. I study law and courts with an emphasis on institutional design, marginalized group representation, and judicial decision making. My research has been cited by the United States Supreme Court, in state and federal court filings, and in state and federal legislative testimony; it's also been highlighted by outlets such as The Associated Press, The Guardian, and The New York Times. Current projects consider the constitutionality of police shootings, qualified immunity in civil rights lawsuits, state court diversification, and the Supreme Court's shadow docket.
