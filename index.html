Greg Goelzhauser

I am an associate professor of political science at Utah State University. My research interests are in American political institutions with an emphasis on law and courts. My current research agenda includes two streams highlighting the institutional determinants of judicial power. First, I am interested in how state-level institutional design choices concerning judicial selection impact who gets chosen to exercise judicial power and how that power is ultimately exercised. Second, I am interested in how institutions incentivize the use of constructive silence on the U.S. Supreme Court through avenues such as agenda setting, the suppression of separate opinions, and constitutional avoidance.
