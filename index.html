Greg Goelzhauser

I’m a professor in the Department of Political Science at Utah State University, where I study judicial politics. My research has been cited in a Supreme Court opinion, state and federal court filings, state and federal legislative hearings, and national news outlets. Recent or forthcoming publications concern constitutional accountability for police shootings, the impact of oral argument attendance, measuring Supreme Court case complexity, and the Supreme Court’s applications docket. I've also published books and articles on a variety of other topics, including state judicial selection and retention institutions, and aspects of Supreme Court decision making such as agenda setting, constitutional avoidance, and separate opinion suppression.
