I am an associate professor of political science at Utah State University. My research interests are in American political institutions with an emphasis on law and courts. I am particularly interested in two questions concerning judicial power. First, who gets to exercise judicial power? This part of my agenda examines the consequences of institutional design choices with respect to state judicial selection mechanisms, evaluating performance in areas such as quality and diversity. Second, when do courts avoid exercising judicial power? Here I study how federal courts use constructive silence by supressing separate opinions, bargaining toward minimalist opinion language, and avoiding constitutional questions to help manage political and institutional pressures.
